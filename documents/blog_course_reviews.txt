SOURCE: Student Blog - "My Honest Reviews of CS Core Classes"

**Blog Post 1: Data Structures with Professor Smith**

Taking Data Structures with Smith this semester. Here's my honest review after the midterm:

The Good: Smith actually explains WHY we care about each data structure. Not just "here's a binary tree" but "here's why trees are better than arrays for this use case." His lectures are organized and he posts notes before class so you can focus on listening.

The Workload: Moderate-heavy. Probably 8-10 hours per week outside class. That's 3 programming assignments and about 30 pages of reading per week. The assignments build on each other so you can't fall behind.

Exams: Smith curves the midterm (this year was +8 points) but the final is straight. His exams are ~80% lecture material and ~20% from textbook. He literally warns us: "I only test what I wrote on the board." Sounds harsh but it's actually fair - his board work IS the exam topics.

Grade distribution: Last semester was roughly A: 30%, B: 45%, C: 20%, D: 5%. Most people get B or better if they study.

Would I take him again: Yes. He's demanding but you actually learn.

**Blog Post 2: Algorithms with Professor Johnson**

Algorithms with Johnson is hard. I'm writing this after the midterm where I got a 62 and it felt like a victory.

Why it's hard: Johnson teaches rigorous algorithm analysis. Not just "code this" but "prove this algorithm works" and "analyze time/space complexity." Her exams are 40% writing proofs, 30% problem solving, 30% code.

Grading: 30% projects, 35% midterm, 35% final. Projects are autograded so it's binary - works or doesn't. That means exams are everything. But here's the secret: she curves. Last year the midterm average was 61 and she curved to 67. The final average was 58, curved to 65.

What's fair about Johnson: She curves the CLASS, not individual students. So if 70% of us got a 60, she adjusts so a 60 becomes a 70. It rewards people who studied relative to the class, not absolute.

Tactics: (1) Read her course notes, not the textbook - her notes cover exactly what's on exams. (2) Go to office hours - she's there 5 hours per week and actually helps. (3) Start projects 2 weeks early - the autograder catches bugs at the last minute.

Would I recommend: Yes, but only if you're willing to put in work. Johnson makes you earn it but she's fair.

**Blog Post 3: Web Development with Professor Patel**

Web Dev with Patel is the opposite of the hard courses. It's actually fun.

Structure: No exams. Instead, you build projects. Grading is 50% projects, 20% participation, 10% quizzes, 20% final project.

Why it's chill: Patel curves at the end (+5 to +10% typically). He cares more about you showing up and engaging than getting perfect scores. Plus he gives extensions without fussing if you ask early.

Workload: Moderate. You're coding a lot but not stressing about proving theorems. Maybe 6-8 hours outside class.

Tradeoff: You learn practical skills (React, Node, databases) but less about the underlying CS theory. That's fine - you get theory elsewhere.

Would I take him: Yes, especially after doing Johnson's class. The mental break is nice and you actually ship projects.
