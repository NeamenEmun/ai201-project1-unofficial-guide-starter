**@student_dave:** Hey all, just finished Smith's Data Structures midterm. If you're prepping, here's what helped: Smith's midterm is curved. The curve this year was +8 points. Average was about 68, so a 68 became a 76. Know that going in - it helps you relax. Study his lecture notes harder than the textbook. 70% of the exam was literally from his board work.

**@CS_major_jane:** Thanks! Does he curve the final too?

**@student_dave:** NO. Final is straight. So actually study hard for the final. The midterm curve is to help people who are struggling early. The final has no safety net.

**@algorithms_survivor:** Just took Johnson's Algorithms midterm. We got absolutely destroyed. The average was 58 (out of 100). But don't panic - she curves everything. I got a 58 and with curve it was a 68. If you're around the median, you're fine.

**@CS_major_jane:** How hard was the proofs part?

**@algorithms_survivor:** Like 40% of the exam was "Prove that algorithm X correctly solves problem Y." Requires understanding WHY the algorithm works, not just coding it. Study proof techniques: induction, contradiction, exchange arguments. That's what separates 70s from 40s on her exams.

**@algorithms_survivor:** Pro tip: Office hours before the exam. Johnson goes through past exam problems. She drops hints about what to focus on.

**@circuits_help:** Circuits with Martinez is kicking my butt. Anyone else getting wrecked?
